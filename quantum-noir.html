ABOUT

I'm Muhammad, a BS Physics student at the International Islamic University Islamabad (IIUI), working under the supervision of Dr. Sardar Sikandar Hayat — a specialist in DFT and materials science.

My research focuses on first-principles electronic structure calculations, using Density Functional Theory (DFT) to understand how electrons behave inside crystalline materials at the quantum level — without any empirical parameters.

I run simulations on Quantum ESPRESSO v7.2 on Linux (WSL2/Ubuntu), visualize charge densities in VESTA, and build computational physics tools like LatticeLab — a Python toolkit featuring Ising models, molecular dynamics, and lattice simulations.

RESEARCH

⚛️
DFT · SCF Calculation

Silicon Electronic Structure

Self-consistent field (SCF) calculations on crystalline silicon using the GGA-PBE exchange-correlation functional and norm-conserving pseudopotentials. Converged total energy, Kohn-Sham eigenvalues, and charge density extraction.

ACTIVE
🔬
Post-Processing · Visualization

Charge Density Analysis

Extracting electron charge density via pp.x post-processing module. Volumetric data rendered in VESTA — revealing bonding character, covalent charge accumulation, and atomic basin structure in 3D.

COMPLETE
🧮
Python · Computational Toolkit

LatticeLab

An open-source Python computational physics toolkit. Features: Ising model Monte Carlo, molecular dynamics engine, and square lattice modules. CI/CD via GitHub Actions, pytest suites, and full documentation.
